# Avatar Styles

Use the matching numbered tile in `../assets/avatar-style-board.png`.

## 1. Soft 3D

Friendly rounded 3D character, soft studio lighting, restrained polish, readable facial features, subtle depth, clean pastel background.

Best for: Xiaohongshu, X, general personal brands.

## 2. Clay Figure

Handcrafted matte clay figurine, gentle rounded forms, subtle fingerprints and handmade texture, soft lighting, minimal background.

Best for: Xiaohongshu and warm, playful identities.

## 3. Flat Vector

Crisp simplified vector shapes, limited palette, clean edges, strong silhouette, minimal shading, excellent small-size legibility.

Best for: GitHub, professional profiles, reusable brand systems.

## 4. Editorial 2D

Modern editorial digital illustration, expressive simplified geometry, tasteful color blocking, slight depth, human-made rather than glossy.

Best for: X, newsletters, creators, thought leadership.

## 5. Anime

Contemporary anime-inspired portrait with clean linework, balanced facial proportions, restrained highlights, and no resemblance to a named studio or franchise.

Best for: Xiaohongshu, entertainment, gaming communities.

## 6. Comic Ink

Bold comic inking, controlled halftone accents, confident contours, energetic but friendly expression, high contrast.

Best for: X, GitHub, gaming, opinion-led profiles.

## 7. Pixel Art

Refined 32-bit-style pixel portrait, deliberate clusters, readable facial features, limited palette, crisp edges, no blur.

Best for: GitHub, developer communities, gaming.

## 8. Textured Paint

Warm digital gouache or textured paint, visible brushwork, imperfect human-made edges, softened color transitions, clear facial likeness.

Best for: Xiaohongshu, artists, writers, warm personal brands.

## Recommendation Heuristics

- Warm and approachable: 1, 2, or 8
- Professional and versatile: 1, 3, or 4
- Technical or developer identity: 3, 6, or 7
- Expressive or entertainment-led: 5 or 6
- User gives no preference: recommend 1 plus two platform-specific alternatives

Do not combine more than two styles unless the user explicitly wants a hybrid. When combining, name the dominant style and use the second only as a texture or rendering influence.
